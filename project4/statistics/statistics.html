Statistics

In 2008, 18.2 percent of the population in Mexico was in poverty meaning they could not buy adequate food for their families even if they used their entire income. An analysis done by CONEVAL found that the states with the highest percentages of food poverty were Chiapas, Guerrero and Oaxaca.

Hunger

In the chart below, the minimum level of dietary energy consumption (also referred to as prevalence of undernourishment) shows the percentage of the population whose food intake is insufficient to meet dietary energy requirements continuously. The data shows the annual change of poverty from the previous year before. For the years 2013-2016, the annual change has been at a constant decrease of -0.20% in Mexico. This conveys how there have been awareness brought to the hunger issue in Mexico, but it has been held at a constant rate for the past years. In order to help make this number be better, people can help to fight hunger in Mexico.
